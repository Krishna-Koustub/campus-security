Frequently Asked Questions

What is the purpose of a campus security surveillance system?
The primary purpose of a campus security surveillance system is to enhance safety and security by monitoring campus activities, deterring criminal behavior, and assisting in the investigation of incidents.
How does the surveillance system work?
The surveillance system consists of cameras strategically placed around the campus to capture video footage. This footage is then stored and can be accessed for monitoring, investigation, and evidence purposes.
Who has access to the surveillance footage?
Access to surveillance footage is typically restricted to authorized personnel, such as security staff and administrators, who need it for security purposes. Access controls and privacy measures are implemented to ensure the protection of individuals' privacy rights.
How can I report suspicious activity?
If you observe any suspicious activity on campus, you should immediately report it to campus security or law enforcement authorities. You can also use the campus security surveillance system to provide evidence of the activity.
Is my privacy protected with the surveillance system in place?
Yes, privacy considerations are taken into account when implementing and operating the surveillance system. Cameras are placed in public areas where individuals do not have a reasonable expectation of privacy, and access to footage is restricted to authorized personnel.
Can I request access to surveillance footage?
Access to surveillance footage is typically limited to authorized personnel for security purposes. However, individuals may request access to footage if they are involved in an incident and require it for legal or other purposes.
How can I provide feedback or suggestions regarding the surveillance system?
You can provide feedback or suggestions regarding the surveillance system to campus security or administrative authorities. Your input can help improve the effectiveness and usability of the system.
